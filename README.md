# Low-Frequency-Voltage-Amplifier

This project involves the design and analysis of a low-frequency voltage amplifier built entirely with 
discrete bipolar transistors and powered by a ±12 V symmetrical supply. The amplifier is designed to
process analog signals in the audio frequency range while ensuring high input impedance, low output impedance,
and stable linear amplification suitable for moderate load conditions.

The circuit architecture follows a classical multi-stage amplifier topology, including a differential input stage,
a current mirror used as a constant current source, an active load to increase gain, and a Voltage Amplification Stage (VAS) 
responsible for the main voltage gain. Frequency compensation is implemented using a Miller capacitor to 
maintain stability and prevent oscillations at higher frequencies.

The final stage of the amplifier is a complementary push-pull output stage built with power transistors, 
providing low output impedance and efficient signal delivery to the load. The design aims to achieve low distortion, 
good thermal stability, and reliable amplification of audio-frequency signals, illustrating fundamental principles 
of analog circuit design and transistor-based amplifier structures.
